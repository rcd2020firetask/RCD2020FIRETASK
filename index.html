Task motivation and description

Motivation

Application of Information retrieval (IR) systems in dialogue based interactive systems has increasingly drawn attention from the research community. IR systems are used to retrieve relevant documents or passages towards constructing an answer for a query/question seeking information on a particular topic during an interactive dialogue between two entities. For example, the image on the right shows an example of one such dialogue with additional contextual information about the entity discussed during the converstion.

Description

The objective of the proposed track is to automatically contextualize two-party or multi-party dialogue systems. We explore the utility of IR systems to retrieve more information about entities discussed in interactive dialogues. To study the problem under a laboratory-based reproducible setting, we propose a number of simplifications. First, since collecting multi-party chat dialogues can lead to individual privacy concerns, items requiring contextualization. Using movie scripts as a starting point, the task requries participants to retrieve relevant information from wikipedia given a dialogue span from a given movie script. The task of the participant is to retrieve a ranked list of documents given a part of a movie script dialogue. The annotated span of text (i.e. the one requiring contextualization) will not be disclosed to the participants. Instead, they need to estimate the information need from the given conversation act. As an example, given the excerpt of the example script from the movie ‘12 angry men’, a simple approach would be to execute the whole script as a query and retrieve a ranked list of documents from a collection. Participants are encouraged to explore different methods to identify/formulate the query from the dialogue and then proceed with the retrieval step. The task itself is that of ad-hoc passage retrieval (albeit without and explicitly entered keyword-style query). The collection from which passages are to be retrieved for contextualization is the Wikipedia collection (dump from 2019), which we will release as a part of the collection. Each document in the Wikipedia collection is comprised of explicitly marked-up passages (in the form of the paragraph tags). The retrievable units in our task are the passages (instead of whole documents).

Guidelines

For this task, we have chosen conversations from movie scripts that constitute situations requiring contextualization. These are long conversations between one or more actors. With the main task of retrieving passages corresponding to a dialogue, we also ask the participants to participate optionally in a stand-alone information extraction (IE) task, where the objective would be to output the span of text corresponding to the information need. A participant could choose one or both of the tasks to participate.

  • Task 1:

    Given an excerpt of a dialogue act as shown in example above, output the span of text indicating a potential piece of information need (requiring contextualization), i.e., in case of the above example, output the text

    Fifth Amendment

    .

  • Task 2:

    Given an excerpt of a dialogue act (see above example), return a ranked list of passages containing information on the topic of the information need (requiring contextualization), i.e., with respect to the above example return passages from Wikipedia that contain information on the

    Fifth Amendment

    .

Evaluation Metrics

  • Task 1:

    For task-1 (information extraction), we will use the overlap of the ground-truth text span (e.g. ‘fifth amendment’) and the predicted text span (e.g. ‘Constitution. The Fifth Amendment’) with the Jacard coefficient measure. Exact match would lead to a Jacard coefficient of 1, and false positives or false negatives would penalize a predictive approach.

  • Task 2:

    For task-2 (passage retrieval), we will use mean average precision (MAP) to compare and evaluate different approaches. This metric would favour systems that retrieve a higher number of relevant passages towards the top ranks.

Task Data

The movie scripts that were chosen to depict situations requiring contextualization involve long conversations between one or more actors. For each movie script, we annotated a span of text that were manually assessed to be indicative of potential contextualization as shown in section above. We selected a number of play-style movie scripts for annotation, i.e. the ones which involve long dialogue acts for plot development. In total, we annotated 4 movie scripts, namely

  • Pi
  • 12 angry men
  • Good will hunting
  • The name of the rose

To facilitate easy participation in the task, we will indexed version of the collection. Currently, we have created a total of 50 information needs (represented as spans of text) from 4 movie scripts. For each annotated information need (query), we constructed a pool of passages by a combination of retrieval with a number of standard IR models, such as BM25, LM, DFR etc. Passages in the pool were assessed by the organizors with respect to each input dialogue.

In the training phase we will release 10 dialogue acts making explicitly available the annotated text spans to the participants by marking them up. The participants can then make use the dialogue in order to formulate queries for retrieving relevant passages.
In the test phase, we will release only the entire dialogue act without the explicit annotation of the precise information need and the relevance judgments (to represent a more realistic situation). The participants are expected to develop and tune their methodologies on the training set and submit the results with optimal configurations of the system on the test set.
